### Key Structural Trend
- Among 897 novel FDA approvals 2000–2024: **57% classified as specialty drugs**; **46% for cancer** among specialty drugs; **55% with orphan designation** among specialty drugs. Specialty share has grown ~3%/year since 2000. ([PMC/Health Affairs Scholar](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12927497/))
- Antineoplastic/immunomodulating agents: **22.6% of all FDA approvals 1980–2022**, **35.8% of all approvals post-FDASIA (2012–2022)**. ([Nature Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-53554-7))

### 3.1 ONCOLOGY

#### Overview
- Oncology is the **single largest therapeutic area**: 29–33% of global pipeline; 35% of FDA approvals in 2025
- 259 oncology novel active substances (NAS) launched globally over 20 years (2004–2024); 125 in past 5 years alone — nearly double the prior 5-year period ([IQVIA Global Oncology Trends 2024](https://www.iqvia.com/insights/the-iqvia-institute/reports-and-publications/reports/global-oncology-trends-2024))
- Global cancer medicine spending: **$223 billion (2023)** → projected **$409 billion by 2028**; U.S. spending grew from $65Bn (2019) to $99Bn (2023)
- 2,000+ new oncology clinical trials started in 2023; 2,162 in 2024 (12% above 2019 levels)

#### FDA Approvals Share in Oncology — By Decade

| Period | Approvals | % of Total FDA | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2000–2010 | ~25 drugs | ~12% | Imatinib (2001) era; targeted therapy dawn |
| 2011–2020 | ~160+ drugs | ~20–25% | Checkpoint inhibitors 2014+; immuno-oncology surge |
| 2021–2025 | ~80+ drugs | ~29–35% | ADCs, bispecifics, CAR-T, oral targeted agents |

**Dominant Modalities (Oncology 2024–2025)**:
- Small molecule kinase inhibitors: ~35% of novel small molecule approvals in 2025; 100th kinase inhibitor (remibrutinib) approved 2025
- Monoclonal antibodies (PD-1/PD-L1, HER2, CD38, VEGF): ~30% of trials
- Antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs): Rising rapidly; 16 total FDA-approved by 2025; HER2, TROP2, B7-H3 lead targets; 80% of ADC trials in solid tumors
- CAR-T cell therapies: ~94 hematologic + 146 solid tumor cell/gene therapy trials started in 2023; CD19 dominant for hematologic
- Bispecific antibodies: ~355 assets in pipeline; 10 marketed globally; solid tumors 69%, hematologic 24%
- Radioligand therapies (RLTs): Prostate (PSMA), neuroendocrine tumors lead
- Novel modalities (cell/gene, ADCs, bispecifics): Now **35% of oncology trial starts** (2024), up from 25% in 2023 ([IQVIA Oncology Trends 2025](https://www.iqvia.com/insights/the-iqvia-institute/reports-and-publications/reports/global-oncology-trends-2025))

- Second-largest TA in trial starts; 7–10% of global trials (2023)
- High failure rate historically (68% Phase 2→3 success rate lower than average)
- Recent approvals in Alzheimer's amyloid-targeting represent a paradigm shift

### 3.3 CARDIOVASCULAR

#### Overview
- Share declined from ~9% (2000–2008) to ~5% (2009–2017) as oncology surged, now recovering to ~8–11% in 2025 context
- GLP-1 agonists are reshaping the cardiovascular pipeline (Wegovy approved for CV risk reduction 2023)
- ATTR amyloid cardiomyopathy (ATTR-CM) is a major emerging focus
- 2025: 5 cardiovascular approvals (11% of CDER approvals) ([Nature Reviews Drug Discovery 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41573-026-00001-z))

### 3.5 RESPIRATORY

#### Overview
- ~4% of global trials (2023); stable but growing in biologic approaches
- COPD newly entering biologic era (dupilumab FDA approved 2024 for eosinophilic COPD)
- IPF: >40 active Phase 2+ trials (2024); still limited approved options

| Indication | Active Pipeline | Dominant Modalities | Key Targets | Notable Recent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Severe asthma | ~25+ active Phase 2–3 trials | mAbs (anti-IL-5, anti-IL-5Rα, anti-IL-4Rα, anti-TSLP), inhaled small molecules | IL-5 (mepolizumab, benralizumab, reslizumab, depemokimab), IL-4Rα (dupilumab), TSLP (tezepelumab), OX40L (amlitelimab), IgE (omalizumab) | Depemokimab (ultra-long-acting, SC 2x/year, FDA Dec 2025 – Exdensur) |
| COPD | ~20+ active Phase 2–3 trials | mAbs (TSLP, IL-5, IL-4Ra, IL-33), inhaled small molecules, siRNA | IL-4Rα, IL-5, IL-33, TSLP, S1P1R, ACE2, KCNK13 (EMP-012) | Dupilumab approved for eosinophilic COPD (2024); AERIFY-1/2 (itepekimab Phase 3 readout 2025) |

## PART 9: KEY TREND OBSERVATIONS

1. **Oncology dominance is entrenched**: Oncology represents 29–35% of FDA approvals and 33% of global pipeline in 2024. This share continues to grow, not plateau. The era of ADCs, bispecifics, and CAR-T is expanding well beyond hematology into solid tumors. ([ISPOR 2025](https://www.ispor.org/heor-resources/presentations-database/presentation-cti/ispor-2025/poster-session-4/trends-in-the-global-drug-development-pipeline-2024))

2. **Specialty drug share exceeds 50%** of all approvals since 2012. Orphan drugs now represent >50% of annual approvals. ([PMC Health Affairs Scholar 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12927497/))

3. **GLP-1 expansion is transforming metabolic medicine**: From diabetes to obesity, MASH, CKD, cardiovascular risk reduction, and now potentially Alzheimer's disease and osteoarthritis. >300 active development programs. ([IQVIA GLP-1 Impact 2025](https://www.iqvia.com/locations/united-states/blogs/2025/11/glp-1-impact-how-glp-1s-are-changing-the-diabetes-treatment-paradigm))

4. **Genetic/rare disease transformation via gene therapy**: CRISPR-based cures for SCD (Casgevy) represent first of many. AAV gene therapy trials have more than doubled in 5 years. >2,000 gene therapies in pipeline. ([PhRMA Innovation Report](https://innovation.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/PhRMA-InnovationReport.pdf))

5. **Neurology remains high-unmet-need but productivity challenge**: Despite >130 active AD drugs in pipeline, the failure rate has been historic. First disease-modifying therapies (lecanemab, donanemab) approved 2023–2024.

6. **Immunology/Dermatology convergence**: JAK inhibitors and IL-targeting biologics approved across RA, psoriasis, AD, IBD, alopecia, vitiligo — same targets, multiple TAs. CAR-T is now entering autoimmunity.

7. **Liver as target organ is surging**: MASH pipeline exploded post-resmetirom approval; GalNAc-conjugated siRNA now routes subcutaneous drugs specifically to hepatocytes. Three MASH drugs may be on market by 2027.

8. **Ophthalmology gene therapy will reduce injection burden**: AMD currently requires monthly-bimonthly intravitreal injections. Gene therapy (4D-150, RGX-314, ADVM-022) could replace this with a one-time treatment.

9. **Biomarker-stratified oncology continues**: NSCLC alone has >15 distinct molecular targets with approved drugs. The fraction of tissue-agnostic approvals (MSI-H, NTRK, TMB) is growing.

10. **Pipeline size doubled 2019–2024**: From 5,584 to 12,203 medicines in clinical development globally — an extraordinary inflection driven by novel modality investment and COVID-era funding. ([ISPOR 2025](https://www.ispor.org/heor-resources/presentations-database/presentation-cti/ispor-2025/poster-session-4/trends-in-the-global-drug-development-pipeline-2024))
