### 13.1 BNT327 (PD-L1×VEGF-A Bispecific) — Competitive Map

#### Mechanism & Differentiation Framework

BNT327/PM8002 (branded **pumitamig**) is a PD-L1×VEGF-A bispecific antibody acquired by BioNTech via its February 2025 acquisition of Biotheus for $800M upfront. It simultaneously blocks PD-L1 (immune checkpoint) and VEGF-A (angiogenesis), creating a cooperative binding effect that theoretically produces synergy beyond simple combination of a PD-(L)1 inhibitor plus a VEGF inhibitor.

**Critical distinction from ivonescimab:** BNT327 targets PD-L1 (not PD-1). Its closest rival, ivonescimab (AK112/SMT112, Summit Therapeutics/Akeso), is a PD-1×VEGF bispecific. This is not trivial: PD-L1 blockade preserves the CD28 co-stimulation signal (via the B7-H1 pathway) that PD-1 blockade suppresses, potentially enabling immune activity in "cold" tumor microenvironments with low PD-L1 expression. BioNTech has explicitly positioned BNT327 as capable of addressing tumors "not generally responsive to immunotherapy (e.g., low PD-L1 levels)," which ivonescimab is biomarker-restricted to serve.

#### Competitor Map

| Drug | Company | Mechanism | Phase | Key Data | Status |
|------|---------|-----------|-------|----------|--------|
| **BNT327/pumitamig** | BioNTech/BMS | PD-L1×VEGF-A | Ph2/3 | SCLC ORR 76.3%; TNBC encouraging | ✅ Phase 3 (Rosetta Lung-01 SCLC, Rosetta Lung-02 NSCLC) |
| **Ivonescimab (AK112/SMT112)** | Summit/Akeso | PD-1×VEGF | Ph3 | HARMONi-2: beat Keytruda PFS in China; HARMONi: 48% PFS reduction post-TKI | ✅ BLA filing intended; OS data needed for FDA |
| **Datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd)** | AZ/Daiichi | TROP2 ADC | Approved | NSCLC, TNBC | Different mechanism; not a direct competitor |
| **Tiragolumab** | Roche | Anti-TIGIT | Ph3 (failed) | SKYSCRAPER-01 failed OS; program largely abandoned | ❌ Effectively discontinued |
| **Atezolizumab (Tecentriq)** | Roche | PD-L1 mono | Approved | Historical SoC; BNT327 directly replaces in SCLC trial | Legacy competitor |
| **Sintilimab + anlotinib** | Innovent/CHIA-Tai | PD-1 + VEGFR | China only | Regional competitor only | Limited ex-China relevance |

#### BNT327 Clinical Data Summary

**SCLC (Phase 2 Global, data cut Aug 7, 2025, presented at WCLC 2025):**
- Confirmed ORR: **76.3%** (38 evaluable patients, 1L ES-SCLC, pumitamig + chemo)
- Median DoR: 5 months; Median PFS: 6.8 months
- Historical SoC (Tecentriq + chemo, IMpower133): ORR ~60%, mPFS ~5.2 months
- Favorable comparison; Phase 3 Rosetta Lung-01 underway vs. Tecentriq + chemo
- All patients achieved disease control

**NSCLC:** Global Phase 2/3 trial (chemo combo vs. Keytruda + chemo) initiated; BNT327 is not requiring PD-L1+ selection — a strategic advantage over ivonescimab's Harmoni-3 trial, which mandates PD-L1 expression. This creates a broader eligible population.

**TNBC (Phase 2 Global, ESMO December 2025):** "Encouraging efficacy" — specific data pending full publication. Phase 3 planning underway.

**Combination strategy:** BioNTech is layering BNT327 with its ADC portfolio (BNT323/HER2, BNT325/TROP2, BNT326/HER3), creating an IO-ADC platform that no competitor currently replicates at scale. Phase 1/2 combo data (BNT327 + BNT325 in PROC) showed 7/13 PRs — highly encouraging for 2L-4L platinum-resistant ovarian cancer.

#### BNT327 vs. Ivonescimab: Verdict

| Dimension | BNT327 (BioNTech) | Ivonescimab (Summit/Akeso) |
|-----------|------------------|---------------------------|
| Target | PD-L1 × VEGF-A | PD-1 × VEGF |
| PD-L1 selection required? | No | Yes (most global trials) |
| SCLC program | Phase 3 (BMS co-dev) | Abandoned (2022) |
| TNBC program | Phase 2/3 planned | Phase 2 only |
| NSCLC program | Global Ph2/3 (chemo combo) | Multiple Ph3 (chemo+mono) |
| ADC combo strategy | Yes (BNT323/325/326) | No |
| FDA OS data hurdle | Not yet reached | Active (HARMONi OS pending) |
| Global development speed | BioNTech claims faster | 4 Phase 3 read already |
| Chinese market | Limited | Dominant (approved) |

**Assessment: BNT327 is differentiated, not me-too.** The PD-L1 vs PD-1 distinction, the SCLC beachhead where ivonescimab exited, the ADC combination platform, and the unselected patient population strategy provide genuine clinical differentiation. The key risk is whether global PD-1 data (Harmoni-3, Harmoni) translate directly to BNT327 — management argues the biologic rationale for PD-L1 is if anything superior, and early data are consistent. Ivonescimab's FDA path is complicated by its OS data requirement; BNT327's global data are still immature. **The market is pricing in minimal credit to BNT327 given BNTX trades near cash value.**

Note: AZ's tiragolumab (TIGIT pathway) failed its primary OS endpoint in SKYSCRAPER-01 and is not a meaningful competitor. Dato-DXd (TROP2 ADC) operates in overlapping indications but via a completely different mechanism; both products are likely to be used in sequence or combination rather than head-to-head substitution.

### 13.2 BNT323 (HER2 ADC) — Enhertu Comparison

#### Market Context

Enhertu (trastuzumab deruxtecan, T-DXd) by AstraZeneca/Daiichi Sankyo has redefined HER2-targeted oncology, achieving approval in breast cancer (HER2+, HER2-low, HER2-ultralow), HER2-mutant NSCLC, gastric cancer, and multiple HER2-expressing solid tumors. As of December 2025, it received FDA approval for first-line HER2+ metastatic breast cancer when combined with pertuzumab (DESTINY-Breast09; 44% risk reduction vs. CLEOPATRA regimen). Enhertu is approved in >75 countries for breast cancer and >50 for NSCLC. Patent protection extends through the early-to-mid 2030s.

#### BNT323 Profile

**BNT323** (trastuzumab pamirtecan, DB-1303) is a third-generation, topoisomerase-1 inhibitor-based HER2 ADC built on DualityBio's DITAC platform. Key differentiating features vs. Enhertu:

| Feature | BNT323 (trastuzumab pamirtecan) | Enhertu (T-DXd) |
|---------|--------------------------------|-----------------|
| Payload | Topoisomerase-1 inhibitor (DXd analog) | DXd (same class) |
| DAR (Drug-Antibody Ratio) | ~8 | ~8 |
| Bystander effect | Yes | Yes |
| ILD risk | Under assessment | Significant (grade ≥3 ~3-5%) |
| HER2-low activity | Demonstrated (DYNASTY-Breast02) | FDA approved |
| HER2-ultralow activity | Being evaluated | Recently approved |
| ADC-IO combo potential | BNT323 + BNT327 (Phase 1/2) | Limited combos |
| Phase 3 HER2+ breast (China) | **MET PRIMARY ENDPOINT (Sept 2025)** | Established SoC |
| Phase 3 HER2-low breast (Global) | DYNASTY-Breast02 ongoing | Dominant |

**September 2025 milestone:** The China Phase 3 (NCT06265428) comparing BNT323 vs. T-DM1 in HER2+ breast cancer met its PFS primary endpoint at pre-specified interim analysis. This is BioNTech/DualityBio's first positive Phase 3 readout, validating the DITAC platform.

**DYNASTY-Breast02** (global, NCT06018337): Ongoing in HR+/HER2-low metastatic breast cancer — data expected in 2026.

#### Is There Room for a Second HER2 ADC?

**Yes, for several reasons:**

1. **HER2-low/ultralow market is massive and growing:** Before Enhertu, HER2-low patients were treated as HER2-negative. The market has expanded dramatically. The HER2-low cancers market was ~$3.5B in the 7MM in 2025 and growing rapidly (multiple approvals in 2025). The addressable patient pool for ADCs is now 2-3x the original HER2+ pool.

2. **Safety differentiation is clinically relevant:** Enhertu's ILD (interstitial lung disease) liability — present in ~15% of patients across studies — creates meaningful room for a competitor with a better therapeutic window. If BNT323 demonstrates a lower ILD rate with comparable efficacy, oncologists will use it in patients who are ILD-risk (prior lung disease, NSCLC patients).

3. **The combination platform is the real prize:** BNT323 + BNT327 (Phase 1/2, NCT06827236, launched April 2025) targets HER2+ and HER2-low breast cancer across multiple expression levels. No approved competitor can combine an IO bispecific with a HER2 ADC in this way. If the combo shows synergy, BNT323 effectively becomes a platform rather than a standalone product.

4. **Endometrial cancer opportunity:** Phase 2 data for BNT323 in HER2-expressing endometrial cancer were positive in 2025; this is an indication where Enhertu is not strongly established.

5. **Market precedent:** ADC markets historically support 2-3 approved agents per target once the indication is validated. Kadcyla (T-DM1) maintained substantial share even after Enhertu approval. BNT323 won't displace Enhertu but could command 20-30% market share in HER2-low given differentiated safety and platform utility.

**Competitive risk:** Enhertu is expanding aggressively into earlier lines (DESTINY-Breast09 for 1L HER2+; DESTINY-Breast11 for neoadjuvant). If BNT323 cannot demonstrate best-in-class efficacy in these early-line settings, it may be relegated to later-line use where patient numbers are smaller. BNT323's China PFS win was vs. T-DM1, not vs. Enhertu — an important caveat.

### 13.3 mRNA Oncology Vaccines — Competitive Moat Assessment

#### Two Platforms, Two Strategies

**BioNTech's FixVac platform (shared tumor-associated antigens):**
- BNT111 (melanoma-associated antigens): Phase 2 with cemiplimab showed 18.1% ORR in PD-1-refractory melanoma (ESMO 2025). BioNTech announced it will not pursue further development in the late-stage refractory setting — a setback. The program's future focus shifts to BNT116 (lung) and BNT113 (HPV+ head and neck).
- BNT113 (Phase 2/3 in HPV+ HNSCC): Active priority program.
- BNT116 (NSCLC): Active priority program.

**BioNTech's iNeST platform (individualized neoantigen therapy):**
- BNT122/RO7198457 (individualized mRNA cancer vaccine): Phase 2 in colorectal cancer (with Genentech). Generates a custom vaccine encoding up to 20 patient-specific tumor mutations. Directly analogous to Moderna's mRNA-4157.

#### Moderna/Merck — The Leading Rival

Moderna's **mRNA-4157/V940 (intismeran autogene)** partnered with Merck's Keytruda is currently the most advanced personalized mRNA cancer vaccine:
- KEYNOTE-942 (Phase 2b, melanoma): **49% risk reduction** in recurrence/death at 5-year follow-up vs. Keytruda alone (HR=0.51). The effect has been stable from year 2 through year 5 — a "maintenance of effect" suggesting durable immune reprogramming.
- **INTerpath-001** (Phase 3, adjuvant melanoma): Fully enrolled, interim data expected late 2026.
- **INTerpath-008** (Phase 3, NSCLC): Ongoing.
- Phase 2 in renal cell carcinoma, bladder cancer.

The 5-year KEYNOTE-942 update (January 2026) is a watershed moment: it establishes that personalized neoantigen vaccines can produce durable, potentially curative immune responses in a subset of patients. This validates the entire platform but also intensifies the competitive dynamics.

#### Competitive Moat Analysis

| Dimension | BioNTech (iNeST/FixVac) | Moderna/Merck (mRNA-4157) |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Phase 3 data | Not yet | INTerpath-001 data 2026 |
| Melanoma evidence | Phase 2 only (BNT122) | 5-year Phase 2b; Phase 3 |
| Manufacturing speed | Proprietary lipid nanoparticle | Proprietary lipid nanoparticle |
| Partner | Genentech/Roche (BNT122) | Merck |
| Keytruda tie-in | No (partner is Roche) | **Yes — locked to bestselling drug** |
| Shared antigen (FixVac) | Yes (BNT111, 113, 116) | No |
| Individualized (iNeST) | Yes (BNT122) | Yes |
| AI/bioinformatics | In-house | Personalis partnership |

**Key insight:** Moderna/Merck has a structural moat in that mRNA-4157 is literally designed to be paired with Keytruda — the world's best-selling oncology drug ($30B+ revenue). BioNTech's iNeST platform is partnered with Genentech/Roche's atezolizumab, a weaker checkpoint inhibitor. Unless BNT122 can demonstrate superiority in a head-to-head or work with a pembrolizumab analog, Moderna has commercial channel advantages.

**BioNTech's counterpunch:** BNT327 (PD-L1 checkpoint) could eventually serve as the checkpoint backbone for its own personalized vaccine combinations, creating an integrated IO + vaccine + ADC platform — something no single competitor has. This platform vision is the strategic moat, not any single molecule.

**Market opportunity:** The personalized cancer vaccine market was ~$303M in 2025 and is projected at ~$12.3B by 2035 (44.9% CAGR). First-mover regulatory approval (expected Moderna/Merck in melanoma adjuvant by 2027) will define reimbursement and patient journey frameworks that second entrants must adapt to.

### 13.4 LEGN/CARVYKTI — BCMA Competitive Landscape

#### BCMA Therapy Class Map

| Therapy | Company | Type | ORR | CR Rate | Key Trial | Status |
|---------|---------|------|-----|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Carvykti® (cilta-cel)** | Legend/J&J | CAR-T (dual-domain) | 98% | 80% | CARTITUDE-1/-4 | ✅ Approved ≥1L (lenali-refractory) |
| **Abecma® (ide-cel)** | BMS/2sevens | CAR-T (single-domain) | 73% | 33% | KarMMa/-3 | ✅ Approved ≥2L |
| **Anito-cel** | Arcellx/Gilead | CAR-T (D-domain) | 96% | 74% | iMMagine-1 | ⏳ BLA accepted; PDUFA Dec 23, 2026 |
| **Tecvayli® (teclistamab)** | J&J | BsAb (BCMA×CD3) | 63% | 43% | MajesTEC-1 | ✅ Approved ≥4L |
| **Elrexfio® (elranatamab)** | Pfizer | BsAb (BCMA×CD3) | 61% | 32% | MagnetisMM-3 | ✅ Approved ≅≥4L |
| **Lynozyfic (linvoseltamab)** | Regeneron | BsAb (BCMA×CD3) | — | — | LINKER-MM4 | Phase 1/2 (1L evaluation) |

**CARTITUDE-1 (5-year data, ASCO 2025):** 33% of RRMM patients remain progression-free at ≥5 years after a single cilta-cel infusion (n=97). Median OS of 60.7 months. Over 9,000 patients have been treated globally. This is the foundation of LEGN's long thesis — durable, potentially curative remissions in a previously rapidly fatal disease.

**CARTITUDE-4 (30-month update, January 2026 Lancet Oncology):** Cilta-cel as early as second-line demonstrated 76.4% 30-month OS vs. 63.8% for SoC. 77% CR rate vs. 24% for SoC. Established OS benefit makes cilta-cel the only CAR-T in MM to demonstrate this.

#### Cilta-cel vs. Ide-cel: Updated MAIC Data (Feb 2026, PubMed)

The most recent matching-adjusted indirect comparison (published February 2, 2026) using CARTITUDE-4 and KarMMa-3 data:
- Cilta-cel: **58% PFS risk reduction** vs. ide-cel (HR 0.42, p=0.0004)
- Cilta-cel: **42% OS risk reduction** vs. ide-cel (HR 0.58, p=0.0452)
- ORR: cilta-cel 1.22x more likely to respond
- ≥CR rate: cilta-cel 1.80x higher

**Conclusion:** Cilta-cel is the clear efficacy leader among approved BCMA CAR-Ts. The safety profile difference (cilta-cel has later-onset neurotoxicity including rare Parkinsonism, vs. ide-cel's earlier but less severe CRS) is the primary rationale for ide-cel's continued use, but the efficacy gap has led to significant market share shift toward cilta-cel.

#### Anito-cel (Arcellx/Gilead) — The Key Emerging Threat

Gilead completed a **$7.8B acquisition of Arcellx** (February 23, 2026), fully internalizing anito-cel. BLA accepted by FDA, PDUFA date **December 23, 2026**.

**iMMagine-1 data (ASH 2025, 15.9-month median follow-up):**
- ORR: **96%**
- CR/sCR: **74%**
- MRD negativity (10⁻⁵): **95%**
- **Zero delayed neurotoxicities** — no Parkinsonism, no cranial nerve palsies, no Guillain-Barré, no immune-mediated enterocolitis
- Safety advantage is potentially practice-changing given cilta-cel's neurotoxicity label

The D-domain binder creates a kinetically distinct interaction with BCMA (binds and releases quickly) that may explain both the efficacy depth and the cleaner neurotoxicity profile. If the PDUFA approval proceeds December 2026, anito-cel will compete for patients where neurological risk is a concern.

**GlobalData forecast:** Anito-cel ~$658M by 2032 in 8MM vs. Carvykti ~$6.3B — suggesting the market views anito-cel as a niche competitor, not a displacer. However, if safety data are confirmed and iMMagine-3 (2L+) reads positively, anito-cel could capture 20-25% of new CAR-T prescriptions.

**LEGN bull case implication:** Anito-cel is NOT displacing cilta-cel; it is likely expanding the CAR-T market by offering a safer alternative for patients who currently receive bispecifics due to neurotoxicity concerns. Total market grows; cilta-cel maintains efficacy leadership.

#### Are Bispecifics Cannibalizing CAR-T?

**Short answer: Complementary, not cannibalistic — with important sequencing nuances.**

Meta-analysis evidence (Haematologica, August 2025, n=1,767):
- CAR-T pooled ORR: **88%** vs. BsAb: **59%** (OR=3.9, p<0.001)
- CAR-T ≥CR rate: **54%** vs. BsAb: **31%** (OR=2.67, p=0.002)
- 12-month PFS: **73% (CAR-T) vs. 67% (BsAb)** — no significant difference at 12 months
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: CAR-T **86%** vs. BsAb **59%**
- Grade ≥3 infections (the true silent killer): CAR-T **17%** vs. BsAb **30%** — bispecifics have **higher** chronic infection risk

**Sequencing reality:** Prolonged bispecific use drives T-cell exhaustion and reduces CAR-T manufacturing quality. Cleveland Clinic data confirm that CAR-T outcomes are best when administered before bispecific therapy. This creates a clinical imperative to use CAR-T earlier, not later — directly supporting LEGN's frontline/2L expansion strategy.

**Teclistamab-dara combination (MajesTEC-3, J&J, ASH 2025):** Hazard ratio of **0.17** for PFS in Phase 3 — described as "the best HR ever seen in a Phase 3 MM trial." 83.4% 3-year PFS vs. 29.7% in controls. This bispecific-daratumumab combo will be used in lines where CAR-T manufacturing logistics are prohibitive, but will increase the depth of response needed at later lines — increasing demand for CAR-T as salvage.

**Net competitive assessment for LEGN:** Teclistamab-dara moving earlier will slightly compress the patient pool eligible for CAR-T in the optimal window, but the 2L+ Carvykti label and the superior long-term durability data keep cilta-cel well-positioned as the preferred therapy for fit patients who want the best chance of treatment-free remission.

### 13.5 GILD (Short Thesis) — Competitive Position & Franchise Risk

#### HIV Market Position

Gilead commands approximately 40-50% of the global HIV treatment and prevention market. Biktarvy (bictegravir/emtricitabine/tenofovir alafenamide) is the world's leading HIV single-tablet regimen, generating ~$12-13B in annual revenue. This position appears structurally secure in the near term due to robust patent protection.

#### Biktarvy Generic Threat

**Generic entry is not imminent.** Multiple sources confirm:
- Earliest generic entry: **November 8, 2036** (based on last-expiring patent 10,548,846)
- The compound has been open to patent challenges since February 7, 2022; no successful invalidation to date
- 10 active US drug patents, 15 total filed from 2018-2023

This is a key nuance for the short thesis: the bear case on GILD is NOT a near-term generic cliff. The patent protection through 2036 is a shield, but it also means Biktarvy revenue is being discounted at a 10-year horizon, and management must prove it can develop Biktarvy replacements in the interim.

#### Lenacapavir Franchise — Credibility Assessment

Lenacapavir (capsid inhibitor) is unambiguously transformative:
- **Science Magazine 2024 "Breakthrough of the Year"**
- **Yeztugo (twice-yearly subcutaneous injection) approved June 2025 as first/only twice-yearly HIV PrEP** — PURPOSE 1 and PURPOSE 2 showed superior efficacy vs. background HIV incidence and Truvada, with zero incident infections in adolescents
- **Once-yearly formulation in Phase 1:** Data from CROI 2025 show Phase 1 PK achieves concentrations exceeding PURPOSE trial thresholds; Phase 3 planned 2H 2025
- Lenacapavir approved in combination with other ARVs for MDR-HIV as Sunlenca (2022)
- WHO included lenacapavir in 2024 recommendations for HIV treatment and prevention

**Lenacapavir + GS-3242 (next-gen INSTI):** This oral/injectable long-acting treatment combination is Gilead's primary Biktarvy successor strategy. Despite the Wonders-2 termination (see below), the GS-3242 pathway remains alive.

**Commercial trajectory concern:** Yeztugo's pricing in high-income markets will be premium ($28,000+ per year), limiting uptake vs. generic oral PrEP (dolutegravir-based generics are pennies in low-income settings). The transformative global health impact does not necessarily translate to proportionate revenue — a key valuation tension.

#### WONDERS-2 TERMINATION — APRIL 8, 2026 (Today)

This is breaking news as of the reference date:

**What happened:** Gilead has formally terminated the Phase 2/3 Wonders-2 trial, which was investigating a weekly oral combination of:
- **GS-1720** (investigational integrase strand transfer inhibitor)
- **GS-4182** (prodrug of lenacapavir, an oral capsid inhibitor)
vs. Biktarvy (HIV viral load suppression)

**Background:** The FDA placed a clinical hold on Wonders-2 and its companion Wonders-1 trial in June 2025, after patients receiving the experimental combination developed decreases in CD4+ T-cell counts and total white blood cell counts. The hold also covered three Phase 1 trials for the individual compounds.

**Current status:**
- Wonders-2 (73 participants): **Fully terminated.** Safety follow-up discontinued.
- Wonders-1 (675 participants): **Still under FDA review.** Hold remains.
- GS-1720 and GS-4182 as a combination: **Effectively abandoned** for the weekly oral regimen
- Lab measures for Wonders-2 participants "returned to baseline or are within normal ranges" but FDA declined to lift hold

**Short thesis support — Why this matters:**

1. **It kills the most aggressive Biktarvy cannibalization strategy.** The weekly oral regimen was Gilead's attempt to preempt generic Biktarvy by offering a differentiated dosing advantage (once-weekly vs. once-daily). That pathway is now closed.

2. **It reveals the difficulty of Biktarvy replacement.** Gilead has been working on Biktarvy successors for years. The CD4 count concern with GS-1720 is alarming — an integrase inhibitor that affects immune cell counts is a significant safety flag for regulators. The FDA's refusal to lift the hold despite normalized labs suggests regulator skepticism.

3. **It creates a 10-year gap.** Biktarvy has patent protection through 2036, but between now and then, Gilead lacks a clearly de-risked oral replacement. The backup (lenacapavir + GS-3242) is early. This leaves the company exposed to any Biktarvy pricing pressure from:
   - International reference pricing/IRA provisions
   - Potential Medicaid rebate pressures
   - Market share erosion from competitor regimens (doravirine, cabotegravir, etc.)

4. **Lenacapavir alone is not enough.** Yeztugo (twice-yearly injectable PrEP) is a breakthrough, but:
   - Its revenue contribution requires premium pricing in high-income markets
   - Global access commitments may pressure Gilead to sublicense generics broadly
   - The total prevention market globally is ~25M people needing PrEP; at $25K/year, this is a $625B TAM on paper, but real-world reimbursement will only capture a fraction
   - Oncology pivot (anito-cel acquisition, $7.8B) is a capital-intensive pivot at the wrong time — deploying $7.8B on a CAR-T with PDUFA December 2026 when the HIV franchise is under pressure

5. **CAR-T distraction.** The Arcellx acquisition shifts management attention and capital toward a competitive oncology market (anito-cel vs. cilta-cel vs. ide-cel) while HIV — which generates >80% of Gilead revenue — faces pipeline uncertainty.

#### GILD HIV Competitive Summary

| Threat Vector | Severity | Timeline | Comment |
|--------------|---------|----------|---------|
| Biktarvy generic entry | Low | 2036+ | Well-protected |
| Wonders-2 termination | Medium-High | Immediate | Kills weekly oral successor |
| Wonders-1 outcome | High | 2026 | Still under hold; unclear fate |
| Lenacapavir revenue ramp | Moderate | 2026-2030 | Yeztugo approved; pricing key |
| CAR-T execution risk | High | 2026-2028 | $7.8B bet on competitive market |
| IRA/pricing pressure | Medium | 2026-2028 | Biktarvy volume/price exposed |
| Competitor (ViiV cabotegravir long-acting) | Moderate | Active | Competing in similar PrEP/treatment space |

**Short thesis verdict:** The Wonders-2 termination today is a meaningful negative catalyst that validates the short thesis. It removes the most credible near-term Biktarvy upgrade path, increases uncertainty about Wonders-1's fate, and highlights that Gilead's HIV R&D pipeline faces unexpected safety concerns that the market had not priced in. Combined with the $7.8B CAR-T distraction, we maintain GILD Short with $90 target.
