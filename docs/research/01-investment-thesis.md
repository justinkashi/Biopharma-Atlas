## Selection Rationale: Why These Four?

Before detailing each thesis, here is the meta-reasoning for why these four companies were selected from the broader candidate set, and why certain alternatives were excluded.

**Why BNTX over GMAB (both were top candidates):**  
GMAB has a +59% SOTP gap and three Phase 3 bispecific programs, but its largest single-day moves over two years were only ±9% — the stock "lacks a binary catalyst that could produce a >15% single-day move" (Prompt 8 event study). GMAB is priced for mediocrity and lacks the asymmetric re-rating potential needed for a differentiated pitch. BNTX, by contrast, has multiple pipeline programs that individually could re-rate the stock by 17–18% on a single day (demonstrated by BNT327 BMS deal, BNT323 Phase 2 data) — and the current price already reflects heavy discounting for the founder departure, making the risk/reward asymmetric to the upside.

**Why LEGN over SMMT (both were early candidates):**  
SMMT is a pure binary bet on ivonescimab's November 2026 PDUFA — it's either a 3x or a zero, which doesn't fit the framework of a differentiated, defensible pitch. LEGN has a cleaner SOTP story, confirmed M&A interest, and a structural undervaluation that doesn't depend on a single binary outcome. LEGN at $18.30 vs. an analyst consensus median of $50 represents a safer asymmetry for the pitch competition format.

**Why IONS over IMVT (both screened well):**  
IMVT is primarily an event-driven play on batoclimab's TED PDUFA (Aug 2026). The mAb event study data (5.14x asymmetry ratio — worst long setup in the dataset) argues against pre-catalyst positioning in mAb companies. IONS, by contrast, is a platform value story with multiple simultaneous 2026 catalysts, none of which alone is make-or-break — the market mispricing is structural, not event-binary.

**Why GILD as the short over VRTX:**  
VRTX at −31% SOTP gap is overvalued, but has a near-monopoly in CF and growing new product launches (Journavx) that provide genuine execution upside. A short on VRTX requires a specific catalyst to close the premium, which may not materialize in 2026. GILD's overvaluation is more structural and increasingly fragile: the Wonders-2 clinical hold termination (April 8, 2026 — today's news) is a concrete negative catalyst, biosimilar pressure on the HIV franchise is accelerating, and the SOTP gap is −46% with no pipeline driver large enough to close it.

## Methodology Notes and Research Limitations

1. **SOTP framework:** All valuations use M&A transaction comps (28 deals, 2023–2026) as per-program anchor values, then discount by probability of success from BIO/QLS Advisors 2020 data. This approach embeds acquirer-level premiums into pipeline values. Standalone DCF valuations would be 20–40% lower.

2. **Current price references:** All prices sourced from [Perplexity Finance real-time quotes](https://perplexity.ai/finance/) as of April 8, 2026 close.

3. **GILD net debt adjustment:** Unlike the Prompt 7 analysis (which presented enterprise-level SOTP gaps), this thesis adjusts for Gilead's ~$20B net debt to provide equity-value SOTP — a critical adjustment that was not made in the summary table. This is the correct framing for a short thesis.

4. **Analyst consensus data:** All analyst price targets and ratings from [Perplexity Finance analyst research](https://perplexity.ai/finance/) as of most recent reports (HC Wainwright, Morgan Stanley, Canaccord, Citigroup, Jefferies, Goldman Sachs, among others). We explicitly disagree with the sell-side consensus on GILD.

5. **Event study modality data:** Asymmetry ratios from Prompt 9 are based on N=3–6 events per modality. The small N means individual ratios carry statistical uncertainty; directional conclusions are robust but exact values should be treated as indicative.

6. **M&A premium inputs:** Bispecific (1 comp), CAR-T (2 comps), RNA (2 comps) have limited comparable transaction data. Small-molecule and mAb comps are more robust (6–12 transactions). Conclusions from limited-comp modalities carry higher uncertainty.
