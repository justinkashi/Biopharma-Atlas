### 1G. Key Risk Factors — Oncology Pipeline Specific (from 20-F)

Based on the March 10, 2026 20-F filing and corporate releases:

1. **Leadership Transition Risk:** Co-founders Ugur Sahin (CEO) and Özlem Türeci (CMO) will transition by end of 2026. Supervisory board has initiated succession search. No named successors as of report date. This is the **highest-rated new risk** this year.

2. **Oncology Revenue Timeline:** All major oncology assets (pumitamig, gotistobart, trastuzumab pamirtecan, BNT324) are **pre-revenue**. First potential commercial launches not until 2027–2028 at earliest. The company explicitly guides to multi-product status "by 2030."

3. **mRNA Technology Ring-Fence:** The new independent mRNA company (to be led by Sahin/Türeci) will receive "certain rights and mRNA technologies" from BioNTech in exchange for a minority stake. The exact scope of which mRNA technologies transfer is **not yet defined** — this is a material uncertainty in the 20-F context. BioNTech retains all current clinical programs.

4. **COVID Revenue Dependency:** Despite the oncology pivot narrative, COVID still constitutes ~70–75% of FY2025 revenues. The structural decline in this franchise is a multi-year headwind.

5. **ADC Competition:** The HER2 ADC (trastuzumab pamirtecan, BNT323) competes in a crowded class against AstraZeneca's T-DXd (Enhertu) and DSP-502. BioNTech-DualityBio plan a BLA filing in 2026; label differentiation relative to Enhertu will be closely scrutinized.

### 1H. Related-Party / Governance Concerns — Co-Founder Departure

**Structure of the Spin-Out:**
- Sahin and Türeci transition to new company **by end of 2026** when service agreements expire
- BioNTech grants mRNA technology rights to newco → receives minority stake
- **No BioNTech cash funding** flows to newco
- Binding agreement expected by **end of H1 2026**
- Both founders remain significant BioNTech shareholders

**Governance Risk Assessment (Moderate):**
The key governance concern is the **conflict of interest** between BNTX's mRNA activities and the new independent company. The 20-F notes BioNTech will retain all current clinical programs. However:
- The technology transfer of "related rights and mRNA technologies" is not fully scoped
- Until the binding agreement is signed (expected by June 2026), there is information asymmetry between insiders and public shareholders
- The timeline — Sahin/Türeci leading BioNTech Q4 2025 earnings call while simultaneously announcing their departure and new venture — created an unusual dual-role situation

**Mitigant:** The Leerink analyst who led Q&A described this as "a logical step for a company maturing toward multiple product launches." CFO Ramón Zapata emphasized BioNTech's independence and pipeline focus. The supervisory board structure (with independent European oversight typical of German SE governance) provides a check.

**Bottom Line on Governance:** This is a yellow flag, not a red flag. The structure appears designed to preserve BioNTech's value while enabling the founders' next chapter. The real risk is execution by a still-to-be-named successor management team.

**Ugur Sahin (CEO) — Oncology Strategy:**
> *"We have advanced our oncology programs with a registrational nucleus advancing in lung and breast, supported by a bold clinical evidence base, with more than 4,000 patients enrolled across Phase II and Phase III studies. **Management Tone on BNT327 (Pumitamig — the bispecific):** **Highly Confident / Assertive**

The rebrand from BNT327 to "pumitamig" (the INN) signals regulatory submission trajectory. Sahin's language was clinical and commercial, not hedged:
> *"Combination approaches are a core part of BioNTech's strategy to maximize the value of our next-generation immune-oncology backbone candidate, pumitamig."*

The BMS co-development deal structure (50/50 cost share; 50/50 profits) with **$7.6B in potential future milestones** gives institutional credibility. CFO stated BMS annual anniversary payments of €613M are locked in for FY2026, providing a financial floor.

Özlem Türeci (CMO) described the pumitamig strategy in three waves:
- Wave 1: Phase 3 registrational in SCLC, NSCLC, TNBC (all initiated in 2025)
- Wave 2: Signal-seeking in additional indications; 8 global Phase 3 trials by year-end 2026
- Wave 3: Novel-novel combinations with proprietary ADCs — **this is the differentiated long-term value**

**On BNT323 (Trastuzumab Pamirtecan — HER2 ADC):**
Management highlighted the HER2 ADC as a potential **standalone registrational opportunity** in endometrial cancer (IHC3+, 2+, 1+ or ISH+). Importantly, Türeci stated:
> *"Where monotherapy activity is compelling and clinically meaningful, we will advance an ADC as a standalone opportunity, and there are in fact a couple of signals which we are encouraged about."*

BLA filing planned for 2026 in endometrial cancer — **this is a potential near-term binary catalyst** (H2 2026 filing → 2027 approval pathway).

**On BNT324 (B7-H3 ADC) — Surprise Positive:**
> *"BNT324 has demonstrated pan-tumor activity and favorable safety profile across a broad range of tumors, categorized by low single-digit rates of Grade 3 treatment-related adverse events and low rates of any grade ILD pneumonitis. One area of particular interest is metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer, where we observe strong activity in heavily pretreated patients. With the goal of moving to earlier lines of treatment, we have designed a Phase III trial in the first line of this indication. We expect recruitment to begin in the coming weeks."*

This prostate cancer Phase 3 announcement was **not widely anticipated** and adds a fourth registrational pathway alongside NSCLC/SCLC/TNBC.

This is **explicit management guidance of ~50% CARVYKTI growth** — a rare and high-conviction statement from a biotech CEO.

### 5B. Manufacturing Capacity — The Competitive Moat

Alan Bash (President of CARVYKTI) provided a detailed competitive positioning update:

> *"There is no other BCMA targeting CAR-T therapy [that] has matched this depth of long-term survival data or overall survival benefit over standard therapies."*

> *"We have the installed capacity to support annual production of 10,000 doses across all our manufacturing nodes."*

**On MagnetisMM-3 bispecific competitive threat (critical analyst question):**
> *"Obviously the MagnetisMM-3 data is good news for patients, but I would remind you... this is a very large opportunity in second and third line. In fact, there are over 100,000 patients, second line plus, globally. That means that there is a significant unmet need and a significant opportunity for the CAR-T adoption in second and third line."*

> *"The IMWG guidelines really help physicians understand that the sequencing here is important, that you wanna get patients as quickly to CAR-T as you can, and that putting a BCMA bispecific in front could diminish the efficacy that you could get from CAR-T."*

**Key Insight:** Management is leaning into the IMWG sequencing guidelines as a competitive moat — bispecifics (e.g., talquetamab, elranatamab) used before CAR-T **deplete CAR-T manufacturing capacity and reduce response rates**. This sequencing advantage is structural, not just marketing.

**On safety (Parkinsonism risk):**
Management presented data from two large academic studies showing that effective bridging therapy **eliminates the Parkinsonism risk** (21 of 22 cases occurred in patients without response to bridging therapy). This effectively de-risks the most significant safety concern that has weighed on CARVYKTI.

There's a lot of transactions recently in the in vivo CAR-T field. We're also thinking ways to potentially accelerate the global development of our own in vivo assets as well."*

This is a soft signal that LEGN is **open to being acquired or finding a strategic partner for its in vivo pipeline**. At the current market cap of ~$3.5B with $1.9B in CARVYKTI sales and peak sales guidance of >$5B, LEGN remains structurally undervalued relative to comparable cell therapy transactions (Bristol, Shire, J&J's own BCMA acquisitions).

**Operating leverage trajectory (CFO Carlos Santos):**
