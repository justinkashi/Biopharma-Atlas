# Biopharma Drug Modality Trends: FDA NME & BLA Approvals 2000–2025
*Compiled from FDA CDER/CBER databases, PhRMA, Nature Reviews Drug Discovery, BCG, ASGCT, and peer-reviewed literature. Last updated: 2025.*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Between 2000 and 2024, the FDA approved **897 novel drugs** (CDER NMEs + biologics), averaging ~36 per year. The decade 2015–2024 saw a sustained elevation: 139 biologics across those 10 years (~32% share), with 5-year rolling averages reaching 46.5 approvals/year by 2024. Small molecules remain the plurality (~64% in 2024), but biologics and next-generation modalities have structurally shifted the approval mix. Seven of the top 10 revenue-generating drugs in 2024 are "new modalities" (BCG, 2024).

## 3. Modality 1: Small Molecules (Traditional NMEs)

### Overview
Small molecules remain the largest approval category, dominating through size and oral bioavailability advantages. The class has evolved from cardiovascular/CNS-focused to oncology-dominated, with kinase inhibitors as the defining sub-class of the 2010s–2020s.

### Key Trend Data
- **2000–2008**: ~80–85% of total approvals; oncology represented ~12%
- **2009–2017**: ~75–80% of total approvals; oncology rose to ~18%; kinase inhibitors expand
- **2018–2022**: ~68–72% of total approvals; oncology ~30%
- **2023–2024**: 62–64% of total approvals per [Nature Reviews Drug Discovery / CCDC analysis](https://www.ccdc.cam.ac.uk/discover/blog/small-molecules-fda-novel-drug-approvals-2024/)
- **5-year average (2020–2024)**: 64.6% of all approvals are small molecules

### Year-by-Year Approximate Count

| Year | Est. Small Molecule Approvals | Key Sub-classes |
|------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| 2000 | 24–25 | Antivirals, CV, CNS |
| 2005 | 18–19 | First kinase inhibitor wave (imatinib was 2001) |
| 2010 | 17–18 | Kinase inhibitors growing |
| 2012 | 33 | First-in-class surge; cancer dominates |
| 2015 | 33 | Kinase inhibitors ~8/year from 2012 onward |
| 2018 | 44 | Record; 23/59 were anticancer SMs |
| 2020 | 38 | COVID antivirals in pipeline |
| 2021 | 36 | Sotorasib (first KRAS G12C) |
| 2022 | 22 | Trough in small molecules |
| 2023 | 34 | Rebound; Paxlovid full approval |
| 2024 | 32 (64%) | Includes peptides ≤40 aa per NRxDD definition |
| 2025 | ~30 (est.) | Kinase inhibitors remain core (100th approved Sept 2025) |

### Key Milestones
- **2001**: Imatinib (Gleevec) — first targeted kinase inhibitor, paradigm shift in oncology
- **2003**: Bortezomib — first proteasome inhibitor
- **2011–2015**: Explosion of kinase inhibitors (MEK, ALK, BRAF, CDK4/6)
- **2021**: Sotorasib — first KRAS G12C inhibitor, target considered "undruggable"
- **2025**: FDA's [100th small-molecule kinase inhibitor approval](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41573-025-00188-7) (remibrutinib, Rhapsido)

### Pipeline Size (2024–2025)
- Small molecules constitute the largest portion of global pharmaceutical pipeline (~22,825 total compounds in development, with SMs comprising ~60–65%)
- Kinase inhibitor class: >200 in clinical development

## 4. Modality 2: Monoclonal Antibodies (mAbs)

mAbs have become the defining biologic modality. From 1 approval in 1986 to 159 total antibody-based biologics through end-2024, the class averages ~10 approvals/year since 2014. Oncology and immunology dominate therapeutic areas.

- Total antibody-based biologics approved globally: 162 (by at least one major agency)
- From 1986–2024: **159 FDA-approved antibody-based biologics** at an average of 5.6/year from 1997 onward ([Oxford ABT 2024](https://academic.oup.com/abt/article/8/3/197/8176596))

## 5. Modality 3: Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)

### Overview
ADCs combine antibody targeting with cytotoxic payloads. After a pioneering (and volatile) first decade, ADCs surged from 2019 onward with improved linker chemistry and payload design. As of April 2025, **14 ADCs** are FDA-approved.

### Key Milestones
- **2000**: First ADC (gemtuzumab) — "proof of concept" but safety issues led to withdrawal (2010)
- **2011**: Brentuximab vedotin reestablishes ADC credibility
- **2013**: T-DM1 (Kadcyla) — first ADC for solid tumor; DM1 payload
- **2019–2021**: **ADC renaissance** — 7 approvals in 3 years driven by improved linker/payload (MMAE, deruxtecan)
- **2019**: Trastuzumab deruxtecan (Enhertu) — high drug-to-antibody ratio, bystander effect; transforms treatment paradigm
- **2022**: Mirvetuximab — first ADC for ovarian cancer
- **2023–2024**: No new ADC approvals but pipeline explosion (22% pipeline growth per BCG 2024)

### Pipeline (2024–2025)
- **22% growth** in ADC clinical pipeline 2023→2024 ([BCG 2024](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2024/new-drug-modalities-report))
- Expanding beyond oncology: 15% of ADC pipeline now non-oncology (up from 6%)
- Deal activity concentrated in ADCs: largest deal sizes in 2023–2024
- Total cumulative FDA approvals through 2025: **14 ADCs**

## 6. Modality 4: Bispecific Antibodies

### Overview
Bispecifics represent the fastest-growing antibody sub-format. From a single approval in 2014 to 15 by 2025, the class exploded between 2022–2024. T-cell engagers (BiTE format, CD3-targeting) dominate, particularly for hematologic malignancies.

- **2024**: 2024 tied as strongest antibody approval year ever

### Pipeline
- 100+ bispecific candidates in clinical trials as of 2024
- Next generation: trispecifics, NK-cell engagers, γδ T-cell engagers
- Deal activity growing but below ADC intensity ([BCG 2024](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2024/new-drug-modalities-report))

## 7. Modality 5: Gene Therapies (Viral Vector-Based: AAV, Lentiviral)

Gene therapies using viral vectors (AAV serotypes, lentiviral) have progressed from theoretical concept to 30+ global approvals. In the US, FDA CBER reviews most gene therapies. The pace of approval has accelerated dramatically since 2017.

## 8. Modality 6: Cell Therapies (CAR-T, TIL, Stem Cell)

### Overview
Cell therapies include CAR-T (chimeric antigen receptor T-cell), TIL (tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte), dendritic cell vaccines, and allogeneic stem cell transplant products. CAR-T dominates the approved landscape for cancer; 2024 saw the first approved TIL therapy.

- 72 non-genetically modified cell therapies approved globally as of Q4 2024
- CAR-T pipeline expanding: BCMA-targeting, GPRC5D; allogeneic ("off-the-shelf") CAR-T in Phase II/III

## 9. Modality 7: RNA Therapeutics (siRNA, ASO, mRNA)

### Overview
RNA-based drugs began with fomivirsen (ASO, 1998) and have evolved into three distinct sub-classes: antisense oligonucleotides (ASOs), small interfering RNA (siRNA), and mRNA vaccines/therapeutics. The siRNA and ASO spaces focus on rare genetic diseases; mRNA therapeutics expanded dramatically with COVID-19 vaccines.

## 10. Modality 8: Fusion Proteins / Fc-Fusion

### Overview
Fc-fusion proteins combine a protein domain (receptor ECD, cytokine, clotting factor) with an IgG Fc region to extend half-life and/or add effector function. The class began with etanercept (1998) and now counts ~12–15 approved products under CDER review.

Sources: [PMC Fc-fusion review 2011](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3225844/); [BioProcess International Fc-fusion review 2014](https://www.bioprocessintl.com/monoclonal-antibodies/immunoglobulin-fc-fusion-proteins-part-2-therapeutic-uses-and-clinical-development); [Oxford ABT 2024](https://academic.oup.com/abt/article/8/3/197/8176596).

## 11. Modality 9: Peptides

### Overview
Peptides (typically 2–50 amino acids) bridge small molecules and biologics. Major therapeutic areas: diabetes/obesity (GLP-1 agonists), oncology, rare diseases, and pain. Since 2000, ~33+ non-insulin peptide drugs have received worldwide approval, with GLP-1 receptor agonists emerging as the highest-revenue peptide class.

## 15. Major Inflection Points and Trend Analysis

### Small Molecules: Steady but shifting
- Remain the majority (~64% of 2024 approvals) but share declining from ~85% in 2000
- Oncology-targeted SMs (kinase inhibitors) grew from ~2 approvals in 2001 to ~8–10/year by 2015–2024
- PROTACs: >20 in clinical trials; no approvals yet as of 2025
- **Inflection**: 2001 (imatinib); 2021 (sotorasib KRAS); 2025 (100th kinase inhibitor)

### Monoclonal Antibodies: The dominant biologic
- Grew from ~1/year (2000–2007) to ~8–10/year (2014 onward)
- **Inflection**: 2011 (checkpoint inhibitor era begins with ipilimumab); 2014 (dual PD-1 approvals)
- Bispecifics emerged as fast-growing sub-class: 0 approvals before 2014 → 8 approvals in 2022–2023 alone

### ADCs: Volatile but accelerating
- "First wave" (2000–2010): 1 approval, then withdrawn
- "Second wave" (2011–2018): 3 approvals
- "Third wave" (2019–2022): 7 approvals driven by new payload/linker chemistry
- **Inflection**: 2019 (trastuzumab deruxtecan/Enhertu transforms HER2 cancer treatment); pipeline now 200+ programs with 22% YoY growth

### Gene Therapies: Delayed but accelerating
- Decades of promise followed by 2017 breakthrough (Luxturna, Zolgensma)
- **Inflection**: 2017 (dual AAV approvals); 2022–2023 (lentiviral/CRISPR burst)
- Cost barrier remains ($1M–$3M per treatment) but outcomes are transformative

### RNA Therapeutics: New platform validated
- ASOs: slow build since 1998; exploded 2016–2021 (DMD, SMA)
- siRNA: validated by patisiran (2018); GalNAc conjugate platform enables subcutaneous dosing
- mRNA: COVID-19 catalyst compressed 10+ years of development into 18 months
- **Inflection**: 2018 (patisiran LNP); 2021 (COVID mRNA vaccines); 2024 (first non-COVID mRNA vaccine)

### GLP-1 Peptides: Commercial behemoths
- **Inflection**: 2021 (Wegovy obesity approval); 2023 (tirzepatide Zepbound)
- Semaglutide/tirzepatide are among fastest-ramping drugs in pharmaceutical history
- Seven of top 10 revenue drugs in 2029 projected to be new modalities, with 5 being recombinant proteins/GLP-1 class (BCG 2024)
