**Key Modality Pipeline Context (2025–2026):**
- Over **230 ADC drugs** are in clinical stages globally as of mid-2025, with **41 in Phase 3**. Top targets: HER2, TROP2, EGFR, Claudin18.2 ([LinkedIn ADC Pipeline Analysis](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/global-adc-drug-clinical-development-2025-key-d3t5c)).
- **2,041 gene/cell/RNA therapies** are in development as of Q4 2025, per the [ASGCT/Citeline Q4 2025 Landscape Report](https://www.asgct.org/uploads/files/general/Landscape-Report-Q4-2025.pdf). CAR-T represents 55% of genetically modified cell therapy pipeline.
- Bispecific antibodies are the "most active experimental zone in immuno-oncology," with hundreds of clinical programs ([Pharm Exec 2026 Pipeline Report](https://www.pharmexec.com/view/pharm-exec-s-2026-pipeline-report-next-frontiers-in-focus)).
- **17 ADCs approved globally** as of mid-2025.

### 5A. Overall Phase Transition Success Rates (All Indications)

Data from the [BIO/QLS Advisors 2011–2020 analysis](https://go.bio.org/rs/490-EHZ-999/images/ClinicalDevelopmentSuccessRates2011_2020.pdf) covering 4,000+ development programs from Biomedtracker/Pharmapremia:

| Transition | Probability of Success (POS) | N (transitions) |
|-----------|------------------------------|-----------------|
| Phase I → Phase II | **52.0%** | 4,414 |
| Phase II → Phase III | **28.9%** | 4,933 |
| Phase III → NDA/BLA filing | **57.8%** | 1,928 |
| NDA/BLA → Approval | **90.6%** | 1,453 |
| **Phase I → Approval (LOA)** | **7.9%** | — |

**Key insight:** Phase II is the biggest hurdle — only ~29% of Phase II programs advance to Phase 3. Despite the popular "90% of drugs fail" statistic, conditional on reaching Phase 3 and filing, **90.6%** are approved.

### 5C. Phase Transition Rates by Modality

From [BIO/QLS Advisors 2011–2020 report](https://go.bio.org/rs/490-EHZ-999/images/ClinicalDevelopmentSuccessRates2011_2020.pdf):

| Modality | Phase I→II | Phase II→III | Phase III→NDA/BLA | NDA/BLA→Approval | **Est. LOA** |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Monoclonal Antibody** | 54.7% | 34.1% | 68.1% | 95.4% | **~12%** |
| **ADCs (Antibody-Drug Conjugates)** | 41.7% | 41.5% | 62.5% | 100.0% | **~11%** |
| **siRNA / RNAi** | 70.0% | 28.9% | 66.7% | 100.0% | **~13%** |
| **CAR-T cell therapy** | 44.2% | 58.8% | 66.7% | 100.0% | **~17%** |
| **Gene therapy (viral vector)** | 51.9% | 38.6% | 50.0% | 100.0% | **~10%** |
| **Protein** | 51.6% | 33.0% | 61.7% | 89.7% | **~9%** |
| **Peptide** | 53.0% | 28.4% | 60.0% | 88.1% | **~8%** |
| **Small molecule (NME)** | 50.6% | 25.6% | 50.6% | 86.1% | **~6%** |
| **Vaccine** | 52.0% | 32.2% | 58.1% | 100.0% | **~10%** |
| **Antisense (ASO)** | 60.9% | 20.0% | 64.3% | 66.7% | **~5%** |
| Biologic (all) | 52.5% | 32.4% | 56.7% | 94.6% | **~9%** |

**Key observations:**
- **ADCs** have a below-average Phase I→II transition (41.7%) but among the strongest Phase II→III rates (41.5%), suggesting early attrition weeds out weak programs; those that survive tend to succeed
- **CAR-T** shows the highest Phase II→III transition (58.8%) of any modality, but below-average Phase I→II (44.2%), reflecting early toxicity/manufacturing challenges
- **RNAi** has the highest Phase I→II transition of any modality (70.0%) but drops sharply at Phase II→III (28.9%)
- **Gene therapy** carries meaningful Phase III→NDA risk (50.0%) — the lowest of any modality — reflecting large, complex trials and evolving FDA requirements
- **Small molecules** have the lowest NDA→Approval rate (86.1%), reflecting CMC and data quality issues

### 5D. Durable Cell & Gene Therapy (dCGT) — Specialized Analysis

From [NEWDIGS/Nature Reviews Drug Discovery 2025](https://newdigs.tuftsmedicalcenter.org/nature-reviews-drug-discovery-clinical-development-success-rates-for-durable-cell-and-gene-therapies/) and [PMC 2024 study covering 995 CGT products 1993–2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12447590/):

| CGT Subtype | Phase I→II | Phase II→III | Phase III→BLA | BLA→Approval | **LOA from Phase I** |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Orphan Gene Therapy (rare disease)** | 55.0% | 49.2% | 68.4% | 100.0% | **18.5%** |
| **Haematological CAR-T/TCR** | 26.3% | 38.7% | 75.0% | 100.0% | **7.6%** |
| All CGT (overall) | 53.3% | 48.4% | 45.7% | 81.0% | **5.3% (overall LOA)** |
| CAR-T cells specifically | ~44% | ~59% | ~90% | 100% | **~13.6%** |
| AAV-based gene therapy | ~52% | ~42% | ~60% | ~85% | **~13.6%** |

> CAR-T and AAV therapies have similar overall LOA (~13.6%), but via different paths. CAR-T struggles early (Phase I toxicity, manufacturing) but has strong late-stage success. AAV struggles at Phase III due to complexity of pivotal trial design and evolving FDA expectations.

### 5E. Biomarker Preselection and Expedited Pathways — Impact on Approval Odds

From [BIO/QLS Advisors 2011–2020 report](https://go.bio.org/rs/490-EHZ-999/images/ClinicalDevelopmentSuccessRates2011_2020.pdf):

| Feature | Phase I→II | Phase II→III | Phase III→NDA/BLA | LOA |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-----|
| **With biomarker preselection** | 52.4% | 46.3% | 68.2% | **~15.9%** |
| Without biomarker preselection | 52.0% | 28.3% | 57.1% | **~8.4%** |

> Programs using patient preselection biomarkers have **2× higher LOA** than those without, driven almost entirely by a higher Phase II success rate (46.3% vs. 28.3%).

From [BLA Regulatory FDA Snapshot 2024–2025](https://bla-regulatory.com/fda-drug-approval-trends-2024-2025/):
- **Breakthrough Therapy Designation:** 54% of BTD-granted products have achieved full FDA approval (317 of 587 granted designations); 38.7% of requests granted BTD
- **Accelerated Approval Pathway:** ~55% of accelerated approval drugs ultimately receive traditional approval
- **First-cycle approval rate in 2024:** 74% (37/50 novel drugs cleared on first review)
- **FDA 2024 approvals:** 50 novel drugs (59 total novel molecules including biologics); 10-year rolling average 46.5/year
